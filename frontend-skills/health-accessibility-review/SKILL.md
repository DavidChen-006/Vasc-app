# Health Accessibility Review

## Purpose

Review frontend work for accessibility, emotional safety, and health-context risk. This app may eventually involve sensitive cardiovascular data, so v1 must be careful even while using simulated data.

## Review Checklist

- Is health data clearly simulated in v1?
- Does the UI avoid diagnosis, treatment, cure, or prevention claims?
- Does the copy avoid shame, fear, or blame?
- Are rest days and recovery pauses treated as valid?
- Are rewards based on safe adherence rather than raw intensity?
- Are colors supported by labels/icons, not color alone?
- Are touch targets large enough for mobile use?
- Is text readable for older users and low-vision users?
- Is critical information understandable without medical expertise?
- Are social or sharing features private/opt-in if introduced later?

## Copy Rules

Prefer:

- "small win"
- "back on track"
- "recovery day"
- "gentle restart"
- "building momentum"
- "your plan"

Avoid:

- "failure"
- "bad patient"
- "weak heart" as blame
- "you lost because you rested"
- "push harder" in a medical context

## Stop Conditions

If a requested change introduces real patient data, hardware data, clinician workflows, AI recommendations, diagnosis/treatment claims, or public health comparison, pause and flag that privacy, safety, and compliance requirements must be defined first.
